Yepip Vulpine

There is nothing more beautiful then the world around us. Pristine life exists just a mile off the path we so often walk. I've always wanted to capture it as it evolves and thrives.

I'm a double major from BYU in Biology and Photography. I've set my sights on studying and documenting the world around us as both a way to bring nature back into the home and help preserve the worlds I explore. As part of that I always give back part of my revenue to support the ongoing effort to save the world around us.

My work often pushes me out into the wild where I spend weeks living out of tents and sleeping under the stars. It's extremely hard work hiking miles to the tops of cliffs, balancing on the edge of waterfalls, and holding still for hours. Sitting hour 2 days waiting for a fox and her kits to come out of a den produced my favorite photo yet. A photo speaks a thousand words, but making one with a story worth telling takes just as many hours.
